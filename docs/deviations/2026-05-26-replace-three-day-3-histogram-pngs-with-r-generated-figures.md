Two data decisions are recorded below.
- **Data decision A — Ford bin-count digitisation** — Neave's two Ford histograms have **no numeric x- or y-axes** — they are qualitative shape illustrations meant only to convey "broad distribution reaching both specification limits" vs. "narrow distribution sitting comfortably inside the limits." The original 50 raw shaft diameters are attributed to Scherkenbach's *The Deming Route to Quality and Productivity*; Neave does not reproduce them. The `(position, count)` pairs in `R/data/day-03-ford-with-compensation.csv` (sum = 49, matching Neave's wry observation on Day 3 page 9 that the printed chart contains only 49 boxes, not 50) and `R/data/day-03-ford-without-compensation.csv` (sum = 50) are a visual digitisation of the printed bars, not a transcription. Bin `position` values carry no physical meaning beyond their left-to-right ordering; both files use the same LSL = 0 / USL = 30 framing chosen so the two charts sit at the same chart width when laid out vertically in the prose and the shape contrast (broad vs. narrow) is directly comparable bar-for-bar.
- **Data decision B — Two-histograms-24-values caption "five" not "four"** — The original PNG's caption and accompanying describe-block stated that both panels "peak at value 13 (**four occurrences**)". The verbatim 24-value sequence in `R/data/day-03-first-process.csv` (transcribed from Neave's printed list on page 7) actually contains **five** 13s — at positions 7, 8, 10, 12, and 13 — and the left panel of the source PNG does in fact show five stacked boxes at column 13 when counted carefully. The new caption and describe-block say "five occurrences" to align with the data Neave printed. This is a small alt-text correction, not a content departure, but is logged here because it is the kind of detail a future contributor cross-referencing source-PDF screenshots could otherwise relitigate.
