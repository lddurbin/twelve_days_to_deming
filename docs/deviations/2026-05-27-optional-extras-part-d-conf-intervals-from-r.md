## 2026-05-27 — Replace Optional Extras Part D confidence-interval PNGs with R-generated figures

- **What** — Replaced the two cropped-PNG illustrations on Optional Extras printed page 50 (the 95% and 99% confidence-region "yellow centre / red tails" bell-curve diagrams) with R/ggplot2 chunks. Both figures are rendered by a new reusable `conf_interval_plot(level, z, …)` helper that draws a standard-normal pdf, shades the central `level`-fraction yellow and the two tails red, drops vertical line markers at the boundaries, and labels the boundaries below the baseline in σ-units (e.g. `μ−1.96σ` / `μ+1.96σ` for the 95% figure, `μ−2.58σ` / `μ+2.58σ` for the 99% figure).
- **Where** — `R/functions/main-functions.R` (added `conf_interval_plot()` directly after `pdf_family_plot()` — these are the same Optional Extras family of helpers and the file is grouped by topic, not by issue ticket); `content/appendix/optional-extras/04-part-d-crash-course.qmd` (two `![…](png){.lightbox}` references replaced with R chunks carrying `fig-cap`/`fig-alt`, `fig-width: 7`, `fig-height: 4`, and `#| lightbox: true`). The orphan PNGs `assets/images/optional-extras/part-d-p50-conf-95.png` and `…/part-d-p50-conf-99.png` are intentionally left in place per the epic-#309 policy (delete only after the conversion has been verified in production — a follow-up PR removes them in batch).
- **Source reference** — Neave Optional Extras printed page 50 (PDF page 56), section "Continuous probability distributions, particularly the normal distribution". The two figures sit under the prose that introduces 1.96σ and 2.58σ as the "particular probabilities that are frequently used in applications of the normal distribution to popular techniques of so-called 'statistical inference'" and are referenced again on pages 54–55 in the discussion of confidence intervals and hypothesis tests.
- **Why** — Inherits the rationale of epic [#309](https://github.com/lddurbin/twelve_days_to_deming/issues/309): SVG output is crisp at any zoom, adapts to dark mode via the CSS-filter mechanism from [#310](https://github.com/lddurbin/twelve_days_to_deming/issues/310), and turns chart edits into one-line code changes. This is the next ticket in the Optional Extras sub-epic [#315](https://github.com/lddurbin/twelve_days_to_deming/issues/315) after [#354](https://github.com/lddurbin/twelve_days_to_deming/issues/354) (normal-family + area-percentages), and the new `conf_interval_plot()` helper is parameterised on `level` so the same helper would serve any future centre-shaded confidence-region illustration (e.g. the 99.8% / "3.09σ" remark Neave makes immediately after the page-50 figures).
- **Standalone helper rather than extending area-percentages** — Issue #355's brief noted the page-50 figures "likely share structure" with the page-49 area-percentages helper from #354 and asked whether the existing helper could be extended. The page-49 figure is structurally different: it carries *four pairs of band-percentages* (34.1 / 13.6 / 2.15 / 0.135) annotated on each curve, expects three stacked panels at varying σ, and has bands drawn in σ-units from the project's existing palette tokens. The page-50 figure is a *single* panel showing a *single* central confidence region in yellow with red tails, no band-by-band percentages, and a boundary-label idiom (`μ ± kσ`) that the page-49 helper does not produce. Extending the page-49 helper to subsume both call-sites would have made it a wide-surface-area conditional function with several mutually exclusive parameter branches; a standalone helper with its own focused parameter list reads more clearly at the call-sites and at the function signature, so the two helpers stay separate.
- **Colour palette deviation** — Unlike the page-49 figure, where the band-tints were drawn from the project's dark-mode-safe palette tokens, here the central "yellow" (`#fff176`) and the tail "red" (`#ed0000`, the project's existing data-ink red) are pedagogically load-bearing: Neave's prose on page 50 introduces these specific colours by name ("a random variable has a 95% probability (coloured yellow) of lying within … 2.5% probability (left-hand tail coloured red)"). The figure's chromatic identity reads as a single coherent illustration only when those two colours are used. The yellow and red are non-trivial under dark mode: yellow round-trips to a dim blue-grey under the invert+hue-rotate filter, which is then re-shaded toward yellow again by the chart's existing colour-token treatment to a workable mid-tone. This is the same dark-mode pattern already accepted on the page-49 figure (red bands among the otherwise-grey/blue palette there).
- **No randomness, no `set.seed()`** — Both chunks are fully deterministic: the curve is `dnorm` over a 801-point `seq()` grid, the boundaries are passed explicitly as `z = 1.96` / `z = 2.58`, and `qnorm()` is only consulted when the caller omits `z`. No `set.seed()` is used or needed; this is consistent with the PR-#377 reviewer feedback that no-op seed calls should not appear in deterministic chunks.
- **Boundary `z` values** — The chunks pass `z = 1.96` and `z = 2.58` explicitly, matching the rounded values Neave uses verbatim in his prose ("1.96 standard deviations of its mean μ" and "2.58 standard deviations of its mean μ"). The helper's default (`z = qnorm((1 + level) / 2)`) produces 1.959964 and 2.575829 respectively — which round to Neave's printed values but differ in the third decimal. For source-fidelity the explicit override is preferred so the boundary tick labels read exactly as the surrounding prose does; the default `z` exists only for callers who do not need to align with Neave's printed rounding.
