- **Why** — Inherits the rationale of epic [#309](https://github.com/lddurbin/twelve_days_to_deming/issues/309): SVG output is crisp at any zoom, adapts to dark mode via the CSS-filter mechanism introduced in [#310](https://github.com/lddurbin/twelve_days_to_deming/issues/310), is screen-reader inspectable in ways bitmap alt text is not, and turns label typos into one-line code edits rather than new crop passes. Two non-trivial data decisions are recorded below.
- **Data decision A — favourite-example daily proportions** — Neave does not print the underlying daily proportions; he himself redrew the run chart from Deming's account. The 31 values in `R/data/day-03-favourite-example.csv` are a visual digitisation of Neave's printed chart on Day 3 page 21, not a transcription. The digitisation preserves the storytelling signal (every point clustered in a tight 8.0%–9.6% band around CL=8.75%, well inside the very wide LCL/UCL=3.1%/14.4% limits) without claiming to be the original numbers. The control limits and Central Line are carried through verbatim from Neave (the limits derive from the binomial p-chart formula with implied daily sample size n ≈ 225, not the moving-range method introduced earlier on the same day; this is why they are so wide despite the tight observed spread).
- **Data decision B — Taguchi loss-function abstract parabola** — Neave's top-panel parabola has no numeric scales (it presents *the shape*, not a scaled function). The R version uses arbitrary x ∈ [-1, 1] and y = x², with the curve, floating axis-cross labels ("loss" / "measurement"), and the word "nominal" placed by eye to match the visual proportions of Figure 34 on *DemDim* page 173. The choice of k = 1 over [-1, 1] is documented here so a future contributor doesn't relitigate why the parabola isn't fitted to Neave's own seven temperature/loss readings — that is precisely the point Neave is making with the two-panel layout. The bottom panel (Neave's personal temperature/loss graph) is data-faithful: the seven (temp, loss) readings printed on Day 7 page 21 are passed verbatim to `taguchi_loss_personal()`, with a `stats::loess` smoother (span 0.9) drawing the curve through them — so any asymmetry in Neave's own readings (he notes that he loses efficiency faster when cold than when hot) comes through.
