## 2026-04-28 — Encode `session_minutes` per chapter from Neave's clock budgets; leave Days 5 and 8 unset

- **What** — Added an optional `session_minutes:` front-matter field surfaced by `filters/reading-time.lua` as a second number alongside the reading-time estimate (`~ N min reading · ~ M min recommended`). The two numbers are framed as independent measures: the reading metric is a literal 200-wpm text-throughput floor; `session_minutes` is the author's editorial allocation including reflection and activities. Encoded the field on Day 1's clean-clock chapters (10 of 13 chapters) using the gap between consecutive clock anchors as the budget. The filter collapses to the single-number reading form when the two values are within ±1 min (combined rounding noise of `ceil` on one side and Neave's 5-min clock granularity on the other) — currently affects `04-resources`, `06-outline`, and `09-different`. Left `session_minutes` unset on Day 5 and Day 8 despite Neave's source having clock data on those days, because the data is editorially unreliable.
- **Where** — `filters/reading-time.lua` (filter); `assets/templates/chapter-template.qmd` and `workflow/PATTERNS.md` (docs); `content/days/day-01/{01,02,03,04,05,06,07,08,09,11}-*.qmd` (10 chapters set). Day 1's `10-relief.qmd` (gap spans the lunch break — would conflate eating with reading), `12-intro-activity.qmd` (last clocked chapter — no next anchor to measure to), and `13-major-activity.qmd` (no source clocks) deliberately left unset. All Day 5 and Day 8 chapters left unset.
- **Source reference** — Neave's printed source uses clock-face icons at the start of each section to anchor it to a wall-clock time in the original 9 am–5 pm workshop schedule. The gap between consecutive icons gives the editorially-intended time budget for the section. On Day 5 (chapters `02-points-7-to-14.qmd` and `03-the-deadly-diseases.qmd`), all 13 clock calls are stamped to `12:00`, almost certainly a transcription artefact rather than Neave's intent. On Day 8, only one clock anchor exists (`9:00`, at the start of `01-introduction.qmd`).
- **Why** — Encoding clock budgets where they are reliable surfaces Neave's own pacing signal alongside the reading-time floor; the implied wpm rate across Day 1 ranges from ~35 wpm to ~240 wpm depending on chapter, so the two numbers measure genuinely different things. The decision to *omit* the field on Days 5 and 8 — rather than emit a calculated number from the broken/insufficient source — is the deviation: we are silently dropping Neave's published clocks where the underlying data does not justify carrying them through. The dual-number indicator only renders when `session_minutes` is set and differs materially from the reading estimate, so Days 2–12 fall back to the existing `~ N min reading [+ activities]` output unchanged.
