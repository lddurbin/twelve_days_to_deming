## 2026-05-27 — Keep Optional Extras Rule 1 short/long control charts as PNGs (no R regeneration)

- **What** — Retain `assets/images/optional-extras/rule-1-short.png` and `assets/images/optional-extras/rule-1-long.png` as cropped source-PDF images rather than regenerating them via R/ggplot2 alongside the [#347](https://github.com/lddurbin/twelve_days_to_deming/issues/347) Day 3 Funnel-track work and the broader [#314](https://github.com/lddurbin/twelve_days_to_deming/issues/314) PNG→R port programme.
- **Where** — `content/appendix/optional-extras/01-part-a-funnel-charts.qmd` (Part A §4 "Control charts for the computer-generated data" → "Rule 1" subsection, ~lines 71 and 75) and the two assets at `assets/images/optional-extras/rule-1-short.png` and `assets/images/optional-extras/rule-1-long.png`.
- **Source reference** — Neave *Optional Extras* Part A, printed pages 9–10 (PDF 15–16) — the side-by-side short/long XmR control charts of his two computer-simulated Rule 1 Funnel-Experiment runs. The text on those pages and into page 11 discusses these specific charts (Central Lines, control-limit positions, baseline-vs-extension behaviour) by their visual features.
- **Why** — Issue [#352](https://github.com/lddurbin/twelve_days_to_deming/issues/352) proposed "Direct reuse of `funnel_track_plot()`", but that helper renders the 20–40 marble-track strip (a position diagram), not an XmR control chart, so it does not apply to these figures. Re-plotting them in R faithfully would require **Neave's original simulation values**, which are not in the repo or the source PDF — Neave only prints the resulting charts, not the underlying 50-value sequences from the two simulation runs. Synthesising plausible-looking series would diverge from Neave's actual simulations and break the visual claims his prose makes (e.g. "Central Line around 30 and control limits spanning roughly 20 to 40"; "the second simulation's Central Line is closer to 29"; "the long version's green extension stays comfortably inside the limits throughout"). The Optional Extras structural brief (`workflow/briefs/appendix-optional-extras-brief.yml`, Part A `figures` block) anticipated exactly this case and set the policy: *"Most are line/control charts — crop from source PNGs rather than re-plotting in R (the Funnel data lives on Day 3 pp.44 & 47; re-plotting here duplicates effort and risks numeric drift). Re-plotting in R is reserved for figures that need interactivity."* These two charts are static illustrations of a closed historical simulation and need no interactivity. The same precedent was applied in [#349](https://github.com/lddurbin/twelve_days_to_deming/issues/349) / [#368](https://github.com/lddurbin/twelve_days_to_deming/pull/368) for the Day 3 funnel-track cutting-out PNG, where the analysis showed regeneration would harm fidelity with no upside.
- **Implementation note** — Both PNGs retained at their current cropped dimensions (rule-1-short.png: 1080×360; rule-1-long.png: 1440×670). Existing alt-text on both call sites already describes the charts in prose for screen readers ("Two short Rule 1 control charts: both baselines show a stable process with Central Line around 30 and control limits spanning roughly 20 to 40…" and "Two long Rule 1 control charts, each continuing the baseline (red) with a green extension to 50 points; both remain comfortably inside the control limits throughout…") and both carry `{.lightbox}` so the alt attribute survives Quarto's image rendering. No `.qmd` or R changes; the only artefact of this decision is this log entry. The other Optional Extras Part A control-chart PNGs (rule-2-short, rule-2-long-a, rule-2-long-b, rule-3-short, rule-3-long, rule-4-short, rule-4-long) sit on the same footing and will follow the same precedent if their parallel issues (#353–#355 in the same series) come up for implementation.
