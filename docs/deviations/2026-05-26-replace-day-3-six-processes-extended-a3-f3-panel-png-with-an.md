## 2026-05-26 — Replace Day 3 Six-Processes extended A3–F3 panel PNG with an R-generated figure

- **What** — Replaced the cropped-PNG figure `assets/images/day-03/six-processes-extended-charts.png` on Day 3 page 23 (Neave's vertical stack of six 48-point charts A3–F3, each showing both halves of the corresponding process with the page-19 control limits *extended unchanged*) with an R/ggplot2 chunk that composes the stack via a new `six_processes_extended_panel()` helper.
- **Where** — `R/functions/main-functions.R` (added `six_processes_extended_panel()` immediately after `six_processes_panel()`, reusing `mr_limits()` and `run_chart_plot()` with `gridlines = "none"` for the clean-axis look Neave's printed page 23 uses); `content/days/day-03/06-six-processes.qmd`; one PNG deleted from `assets/images/day-03/`. - **Source reference** — Neave Day 3 page 23. The pedagogical point at the foot of page 22 governs the construction: "it saves you time and effort by not recomputing the control limits when there is no need to do so. Second, if a future recomputation of control limits is being carried out while the process is becoming unstable, you'll obviously be spoiling the control chart's chance of warning you about that particular problem ... So the clear 'no-brainer' message is to leave the control limits alone if you have no good reason for changing them!"
- **Why** — Inherits the rationale of epic [#309](https://github.com/lddurbin/twelve_days_to_deming/issues/309) and parent [#313](https://github.com/lddurbin/twelve_days_to_deming/issues/313): SVG output is crisp at any zoom, adapts to dark mode via the CSS-filter mechanism from [#310](https://github.com/lddurbin/twelve_days_to_deming/issues/310), and the live-computed limits stay in lockstep with the page-19 limits (since both derive from the same `process_X1` data through `mr_limits()`). The page-19 ↔ page-23 internal consistency Neave's prose relies on holds by construction rather than by manual coordination of two cropped PNGs.
- **Implementation note — limits from first half only** — The helper concatenates `process_X1` (the in-control 24 points) with `process_X2` (the contaminated 24 points), then computes MR-based limits from `process_X1` alone and draws those limits across all 48 points. This is the geometric expression of Neave's "no recomputation" rule: the limits sit where the stable-period data put them and let the second-half points breach them where the special cause appears.
