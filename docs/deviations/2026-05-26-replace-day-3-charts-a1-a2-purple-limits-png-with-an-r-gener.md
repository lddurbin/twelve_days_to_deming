## 2026-05-26 — Replace Day 3 Charts A1 / A2 (purple-limits) PNG with an R-generated figure

- **What** — Replaced the cropped-PNG figure `assets/images/day-03/charts-a1-a2-purple-limits.png` on Day 3 page 15 (the two control charts showing standard-deviation-based limits in magenta, the first half of Neave's six-processes / page-19 contrast) with an R/ggplot2 chunk that simulates Neave's dice recipe and computes the limits live.
- **Where** — `R/data/day-03-six-processes.R` (new — defines `process_A1`, `process_A2`); `R/functions/main-functions.R` (added `hline_colour` parameter to `run_chart_plot()`, defaulting to blue so every existing call site is unchanged); `content/days/day-03/05-how-do-we-compute-control-limits.qmd`; one PNG deleted from `assets/images/day-03/`.
- **Source reference** — Neave Day 3 page 15 (single figure) and page 19 (the full six-processes panel from which it is excerpted). Process A is described in the page-19 prose as "the total 'spots' showing when four dice are thrown" (24 throws); the A2 special-cause recipe is "four dice were used in the first six throws ... but only two dice were used in the next six throws and then six dice for the rest of the time."
- **Why** — Inherits the rationale of epic [#309](https://github.com/lddurbin/twelve_days_to_deming/issues/309) and child [#313](https://github.com/lddurbin/twelve_days_to_deming/issues/313): SVG output is crisp at any zoom, adapts to dark mode via the CSS-filter mechanism from [#310](https://github.com/lddurbin/twelve_days_to_deming/issues/310), and turns chart edits (or the swap from purple-limits to blue-limits across pages 15 ↔ 19 — to be carried out under [#344](https://github.com/lddurbin/twelve_days_to_deming/issues/344)) into one-line code changes rather than new crop passes. Two non-trivial data decisions are recorded below.
- **Data decision A — Process A dice data is simulated, not transcribed** — Neave does not print the original throw-by-throw dice totals; he simulated them himself when preparing the source material in the 1980s and reproduced the resulting charts. The 48 values in `R/data/day-03-six-processes.R` are re-simulated under `set.seed(274)` so the visual shape of A1 (24 four-dice totals fluctuating around ≈14) and the special-cause shape of A2 (six four-dice totals around ≈14, then six two-dice totals around ≈7, then twelve six-dice totals around ≈21) both reproduce Neave's published charts. The seed is not arbitrary: it was chosen by a sweep over seeds 1–300 that minimised the gap between the two pedagogical claims Neave's prose makes about page 15: "those purple limits on this Chart A1 (computed using standard deviations) are virtually the same as the blue ones (computed using moving ranges)" (under seed 274, A1's 3σ ≈ 8.72 vs. 2.66·MR̄ ≈ 8.56 — agreement to within 2%) and the page-19 limits on A2 "are approximately twice as far apart" (under seed 274, A2's 3σ ≈ 22.52 vs. 2.66·MR̄ ≈ 11.22 — ratio 2.01). The blue ("page 19") limits will be computed from the same dataset when the six-processes panel chart is implemented under [#344](https://github.com/lddurbin/twelve_days_to_deming/issues/344), so the page-15 ↔ page-19 internal consistency Neave's prose relies on continues to hold across the two pages.
- **Data decision B — "purple" rendered as magenta** — Neave prints the contrasting limits in a saturated pink-purple. The R chunk uses pure magenta (`#ff00ff`), which is the colour-wheel point that survives the dark-mode `invert + hue-rotate(180deg)` filter from [#310](https://github.com/lddurbin/twelve_days_to_deming/issues/310) cleanly (invert flips magenta to green; the 180° hue-rotate sends green back to magenta). The qmd prose, the figure caption, and the describe-block were updated to read "magenta" rather than "purple" so the alt-text matches what the reader sees in either mode. Neave's contrast with the blue moving-range limits on page 19 is unchanged.
