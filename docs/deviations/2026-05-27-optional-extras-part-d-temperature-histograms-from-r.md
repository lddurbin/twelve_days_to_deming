## 2026-05-27 — Replace eight Optional Extras Part D body-temperature histogram PNGs with R-generated figures

- **What** — Replaced the eight cropped-PNG body-temperature histograms on printed pages 44–46 with R/ggplot2 chunks generated inline by a new `histogram_with_pdf()` helper. Each chunk draws a histogram of `n` simulated body-temperature readings from N(36.8, 0.35²) (Neave's stated parameters), rounded to the printed precision, with a smooth normal density overlaid in blue. The eight chunks cover the same `(n, precision)` ladder Neave printed: 1k integer, 10k integer, 1k 1-dp, 10k 1-dp, 10k 2-dp, 100k 2-dp, 1m 2-dp, 10m 2-dp — the convergence sequence that climaxes in the recognisable bell shape.
- **Where** — `R/functions/main-functions.R` (added `histogram_with_pdf()`); `content/appendix/optional-extras/04-part-d-crash-course.qmd` (eight `![…](png){.lightbox}` references replaced with R chunks carrying `fig-cap`/`fig-alt`). The orphan PNGs in `assets/images/optional-extras/part-d-p44-temps-*.png`, `part-d-p45-temps-*.png`, and `part-d-p46-temps-10m-2dp.png` are intentionally left in place per the epic-#309 policy (delete only after the conversion has been verified in production).
- **Source reference** — Neave Optional Extras printed pages 44–46 (PDF pages 50–52), section "Linking it all together" (continued from page 43). The accompanying prose on page 46 ends "I was in fact generating data from N(36.8, 0.35²)" — the figures here are reproduced from those same parameters.
- **Why** — Inherits the rationale of epic [#309](https://github.com/lddurbin/twelve_days_to_deming/issues/309) (SVG output is crisp at any zoom, adapts to dark mode via the CSS-filter mechanism from [#310](https://github.com/lddurbin/twelve_days_to_deming/issues/310), and turns chart edits into one-line code changes). This is the first ticket in the Optional Extras sub-epic [#315](https://github.com/lddurbin/twelve_days_to_deming/issues/315) and introduces the `histogram_with_pdf()` helper that [#354](https://github.com/lddurbin/twelve_days_to_deming/issues/354) (normal-family + area-percentages diagrams) and [#358](https://github.com/lddurbin/twelve_days_to_deming/issues/358) (Part E PDFs + x-bar histograms) will reuse.
- **Reproducibility — seeds and parameters** — Every chunk calls `set.seed(353)` immediately before its single `rnorm()` draw, so each figure is independently reproducible regardless of chunk evaluation order. The seed value `353` is the GitHub issue number, chosen so future maintainers can rederive it without consulting this log. All eight chunks draw from `rnorm(n, mean = 36.8, sd = 0.35)` (Neave's stated parameters from the next page of prose); the precision per chunk follows Neave's `digits` ladder (0, 0, 1, 1, 2, 2, 2, 2).
- **What is NOT being claimed** — The simulated samples here are **not** the same samples Neave generated for the original PDF. Neave does not publish his seed or his RNG implementation, so the per-bin counts will not match his figures exactly (e.g. with `set.seed(353)` the 1,000-integer sample yields counts of 196/785/19 across 36/37/38, whereas Neave's printed proportions of 0.176/0.801/0.023 imply counts of 176/801/23 from his sample). Neave's *pedagogical claim* is about the limiting shape, not the per-bin counts — the convergence to the smooth bell curve as `n` grows and precision tightens is what these figures illustrate, and that claim is preserved exactly by drawing independently from the same N(36.8, 0.35²) population.
