## 2026-05-27 — Replace Optional Extras Part E subgroup illustrations (12 and 40 subgroups) with R-generated figures

- **What** — Replaced the two cropped-PNG figures on Part E pages 69–70 with R/ggplot2 chunks: `part-e-p69-12-subgroups.png` (three stacked histograms of the simulated false-signal probability on an *X̄*-chart constructed from 12 subgroups of size 2, 4 and 6 respectively, with the textbook target 0.0027 marked in red on each axis) and `part-e-p70-40-subgroups.png` (the same three histograms but with 40 subgroups instead of 12). Both chunks are built on a new `xbar_false_signal_probs()` simulation helper and a sibling `xbar_false_signal_panel()` plot helper.
- **Where** — `content/appendix/optional-extras/05-part-e-normality.qmd` (the two PNG references replaced with `fig-part-e-12-subgroups` and `fig-part-e-40-subgroups` R chunks); `R/functions/main-functions.R` (new `xbar_false_signal_probs()` and `xbar_false_signal_panel()`); `tests/testthat/test-distribution-helpers.R` (new test cases). The orphan PNGs in `assets/images/optional-extras/part-e-p69-12-subgroups.png` and `assets/images/optional-extras/part-e-p70-40-subgroups.png` are intentionally left in place per the epic-#309 policy (delete only after the conversion has been verified in production).
- **Source reference** — Neave Optional Extras Part E pages 69 (12-subgroup figure) and 70 (40-subgroup figure). The intro on pages 63–64 ("the second computer simulation") describes the construction: simulate subgroups from a normal distribution; for each replication, build a standard *X̄*-chart from the simulated baseline using the conversion factors from [Part B (pages 21–22)](02-part-b-subgrouped.qmd#sec-optional-extras-page-20); compute the probability that a subsequent value of *X̄* falls outside those limits; collect all probabilities into a histogram. Page 69 prose specifies "12 subgroups of size 4" (extended here to subgroup sizes 2 and 6 as on page 69's figure), one million replications, x-axis "from zero probability right up to 0.025"; page 70 prose specifies "40 subgroups instead". Both pages mark 0.0027 in red on each axis.
- **Why** — Inherits the rationale of epic [#309](https://github.com/lddurbin/twelve_days_to_deming/issues/309) and the Optional Extras sub-epic [#315](https://github.com/lddurbin/twelve_days_to_deming/issues/315). SVG output is crisp at any zoom, adapts to dark mode via the CSS-filter mechanism from [#310](https://github.com/lddurbin/twelve_days_to_deming/issues/310), and the simulation is now reproducible from a fixed seed rather than a one-off PDF crop.
- **Helper-reuse vs new-design call** — Adopted a new pair of helpers rather than reusing the page-66/67 (`pdf_family_plot()` and the inline patchwork code from [#358](https://github.com/lddurbin/twelve_days_to_deming/issues/358)) work. The page-67 simulation produces *MR̄* ÷ 1.128 estimates of σ from one-at-a-time data (a univariate simulation with a closed-form estimator); pages 69/70 produce X-bar-chart false-signal probabilities from subgrouped data (which require computing X̿ ± A₂ · R̄ and then a normal-tail probability per replication). The two simulations share no compute primitives. The page-67 histograms also share a fixed x-axis from 0 to 2.5 and a subtitle-style label *above* each panel; the page-69/70 histograms differ on every front (varying x-axis upper bound between 0.020 and 0.025, x-axis tick labels at every 0.005, a red 0.0027 tick *below* the baseline, a caption-style label *under* each panel). Forcing those two layouts behind a shared helper would have widened it on every dimension while sharing none of the underlying computation. Two narrow helpers each does one thing.
- **Parametrisations** — Standard X-bar chart construction: \eqn{UCL/LCL = \bar{\bar{X}} \pm A_2 \bar{R}} with \eqn{A_2 = 3 / (d_2 \sqrt{n})} and \eqn{d_2 \in \{1.128, 1.693, 2.059, 2.326, 2.534\}} for \eqn{n \in \{2, 3, 4, 5, 6\}} respectively (the standard Part B page 20 values). Subgroups drawn from N(0, 1). For each replication the false-signal probability is computed via the closed-form normal-tail formula \eqn{P(\bar{X} < LCL) + P(\bar{X} > UCL)} where the future subgroup mean is N(0, 1/√n).
- **Simulation parameters** — N = 200,000 replications per `(n, m_subgroups)` combination (six simulations in total, three per page), with `set.seed(359)` (page 69) and `set.seed(360)` (page 70) before the per-page bundle of three draws. Neave used 1,000,000. 200,000 is enough to make the visual claims of the two pages read obviously — that the page-69 histograms spread well past 0.0027 even at the highest subgroup size, and that the page-70 histograms tighten dramatically when the baseline grows from 12 to 40 — and avoids dragging down per-render Quarto times. Larger N produces visually indistinguishable histograms on a printed page.
- **Layout deviations from the printed page** — Two cosmetic differences:
  - The printed figure on page 69 places `0.000` to `0.020` on the axis but extends slightly past `0.020` (the rightmost label is `0.02` on the page). The R chunk uses a hard limit of `x_max = 0.020` so the axis ends cleanly at 0.020; the long right tails (visible in the printed page as occasional bars past 0.020 on the n = 2 panel) are clipped onto the rightmost bin so the histogram does not under-count the tail.
  - The 0.0027 red tick on the printed page is a short tick *with no surrounding axis ticks*. The R chunk includes 0.005-spaced axis ticks (black) underneath the histogram for readability, with the 0.0027 tick rendered in red just below the baseline (linewidth 1.2 to stand out from the slimmer regular ticks).
- **Colour deviations** — None new: black axes and white-filled / black-outlined bars matching Neave's printed page, and `CHART_LINE_COLOUR` (the existing project red) for the 0.0027 tick and label.
