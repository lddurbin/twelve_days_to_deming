## 2026-05-28 — Replace Optional Extras Part D CLT-demo exponential PNGs with R-generated figures

Completes the Part D CLT-demo conversion started for the uniform and triangular parents in PR [#380](https://github.com/lddurbin/twelve_days_to_deming/pull/380) under issue [#356](https://github.com/lddurbin/twelve_days_to_deming/issues/356).
- **Where** — `content/appendix/optional-extras/04-part-d-crash-course.qmd` (five inline-image references replaced by R chunks; the orphan PNGs in `assets/images/optional-extras/` remain on disk per the epic-[#309](https://github.com/lddurbin/twelve_days_to_deming/issues/309) policy and will be removed in a follow-up PR after production verification).
- **Source reference** — Neave Optional Extras Part D pages 53–54. The page-51 prose introduces the simulation context ("samples were generated 10,000,000 times") and pages 53–54 contain the five figure captions verbatim reproduced as `fig-cap` strings in the new chunks. Neave's exponential parametrisation is not stated numerically — the figure is a shape illustration — so the choice below derives from the σ = 1 constraint his prose specifies (and which Part E pages 65–67 then explicitly reuse "for ease of comparison with the initial normal version").
- **Why** — Inherits the rationale of epic [#309](https://github.com/lddurbin/twelve_days_to_deming/issues/309) and the Optional Extras sub-epic [#315](https://github.com/lddurbin/twelve_days_to_deming/issues/315). SVG output is crisp at any zoom, adapts to dark mode via the CSS-filter mechanism from [#310](https://github.com/lddurbin/twelve_days_to_deming/issues/310), and the simulation is now reproducible from a fixed seed.
- **Parametrisation (σ = 1)** — **Exponential**: rate 1 (`rexp(n, rate = 1)`), giving mean μ = 1 and standard deviation σ = 1. This matches the σ = 1 family-wide convention established for the uniform and triangular parents in PR [#380](https://github.com/lddurbin/twelve_days_to_deming/pull/380).
- **Simulation parameters** — `n_samples = 100000` standardised *X̄* values per histogram (helper default), with `set.seed(357)` at the top of each random-draw chunk (one seed per sampling chunk; the PDF chunk is deterministic and carries no seed). Neave used 10,000,000; 100,000 is comfortably enough to make the CLT shape claims visually obvious on a printed/screen page and keeps per-chunk render time well under a second. Larger N produces visually indistinguishable histograms at the printed resolution.
- **Helper reuse — `clt_demo_plot()`** — No new helper added. The four sampling-distribution chunks call `clt_demo_plot()` (added to `R/functions/main-functions.R` in PR [#379](https://github.com/lddurbin/twelve_days_to_deming/pull/379) and landed alongside PR [#380](https://github.com/lddurbin/twelve_days_to_deming/pull/380)) with `parent_dist = function(n) rexp(n, rate = 1)` and `mu = 1`, `sigma = 1`. This is the exact reuse path the helper was designed for and called out in its roxygen docs.
- **PDF (decorative) chunk** — The exponential parent-distribution PDF is drawn as a bespoke shape illustration in the chunk (not via `pdf_family_plot()`), matching the structure of the page-52 `fig-uniform-pdf` and `fig-tri-pdf` chunks introduced in PR [#380](https://github.com/lddurbin/twelve_days_to_deming/pull/380). The decreasing-exponential `dexp(x, rate = 1)` curve is evaluated on a fixed grid and rendered as a thin black outline over a horizontal baseline with no axis ink — Neave's printed page has no axes. The chunk is deterministic and carries no `set.seed()` call.
